# PRODUCT.md — EG Page Devis

## Product Purpose

A personalized two-page system for landscaping businesses in French-speaking Switzerland, deployed as a cold prospecting gift on `eg.jonlabs.ch`. Each prospect gets:

- **`/landing/[slug]`** — minimal sales page (H1, screenshot of their mocked site, CTA, generic Loom case study video) that drives clicks to their site mock.
- **`/site/[slug]`** — full mocked landscaper website (9 sections: hero with transparency note, local market stats, services, intervention zones, testimonials, gallery, qualifying quiz, SEO education, final CTA).

Each page is configured per prospect (name, colors, photos, services, communes, local market data) and includes a multi-step consultation quiz that sends qualified leads by email. The goal is to demonstrate Jon Labs' value before any sales conversation and drive prospect-to-call conversion (target: 20%).

## Register

brand — the design IS the product. The page must seduce, establish trust, and guide, not just collect data. The prospect (paysagiste) will show this page to their own clients. It must look better than anything they could build themselves.

## Users

**Primary: The homeowner (final client)**

- 40–65 years old, villa or house with garden in the Geneva/Vaud/Fribourg area
- Comfortable income, attentive to quality and aesthetics
- Skeptical about price (landscaping is perceived as expensive)
- Anxious: "Will they really show up? Will they understand what I want?"
- Deep desire: a garden they're proud of, a space to entertain guests
- Not filling a form — they're describing a project to someone who will care for it
- Decision cycle is long; they compare 2–3 artisans; seasonal timing matters

**Secondary: The landscaper (prospect / page owner)**

- Small business owner, 1–10 employees
- Receives leads by email, calls back within 48h
- Values trust, punctuality, word-of-mouth reputation
- Will show this page as a portfolio piece to his clients

## Brand

**Jon Labs** (creator, single discreet footer mention)
**GT Paysages Sàrl** (first deployed instance, Genève)

Tone: warm, expert, human, unhurried. Not startup-bubbly. Not corporate-formal. Like a trusted local artisan who happens to have an exceptional digital presence.

Voice:

- Direct ("Votre jardin mérite un expert")
- Personalized (use first name after contact step)
- No hedging ("peut-être", "pourrait", "essayez")
- No SaaS enthusiasm ("Découvrez notre incroyable solution!")
- Editorial confidence — state things, don't pitch them

## Strategic Principles

1. The quote IS the hook. Showing a real price range (CHF X – CHF Y) before any competitor does is the differentiator. Don't bury it.
2. The quiz is a guided consultation, not a form. Language, pacing, and visual treatment must reinforce this distinction.
3. Trust signals matter enormously in the Swiss artisan market: years of experience, testimonials, service zone coverage.
4. The page is a gift — the landscaper didn't pay for it yet. It must impress enough that they want to.
5. Every step reduces friction. If a step feels bureaucratic, it's wrong.

## Anti-References

- Generic quote SaaS (Typeform-style, cold blue/white, "Fill in the fields")
- Real estate lead gen pages (aggressive CTAs, stock photography of happy couples)
- French contractor sites (busy, multiple fonts, clipart icons, green/orange)
- Dark-mode "tech tool" aesthetic
- Hero-metric templates (big number + stats + gradient)
- Any page that feels designed for 1000 users instead of this one person
